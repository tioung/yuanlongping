袁隆平

Chinese national hero

The father of hybrid rice

袁隆平 (Yuan Longping) was born in Beijing, China on September 7, 1930. His ancestral home is in De'an County, Jiujiang, Jiangxi Province in Southern China.

>Hybridizing rice to increase its yield had a problem: rice is a self-pollinating plant but hybridization requires separating male and female plants as parents, which was not feasible on a large scale.

In 1961 袁隆平 spotted a seed-head of wild hybrid rice and by 1964, he hypothesized that naturally-mutated male-sterile rice could exist. Two years later he reported in a scientific publication that he had found a few individuals of male-sterile rice that could be used for the production of hybrid rice.

The first experimental hybrid rice did not show any significant advantage over commonly grown varieties, so 袁隆平 suggested crossbreeding cultivated rice varieties with ones growing wild in the countryside. In 1970, beside a railway line in Hainan, he and his team found a particularly important wild variety.

Using this one within a breeding programme resulted in varieties with yields improved by 20 - 30 % in the late 1970s, which in a country that always had to grapple with famine and starvation, was of no small significance. For this achievement, 袁隆平 was dubbed the "Father of Hybrid Rice".

Four asteroids and a college in China have been named after him. The minor planet 8117 Yuanlongping was also named after 袁隆平. He also won the State Preeminent Science and Technology Award of China in 2000, the Wolf Prize in Agriculture and the World Food Prize in 2004.

On May 22, 2021, 袁隆平 died of multiple organ failure at Xiangya Hospital of Central South University and a nation wide mourning rarely seen in recent years started.
